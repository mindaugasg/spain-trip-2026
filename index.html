Sights & Culture

Sight

Mezquita-Catedral 📍

★★★★★ 4.8 Google · 4.5 Tripadvisor

€13 · Book ahead at mezquitadecordoba.org

UNESCO World Heritage mosque-cathedral with 856 red-and-white horseshoe arches. Patio de los Naranjos (orange courtyard) is free. One of the most extraordinary buildings in Europe.

Sight

Roman Bridge 📍

★★★★☆ 4.6 Google · 4.5 Tripadvisor

Free · Open 24h

230m flat walk across the Guadalquivir with spectacular Mezquita views. Benches at both ends. Best at sunset when the stone glows golden. Pregnancy-perfect — completely flat.

Sight

★★★★☆ 4.5 Google · 4.5 Tripadvisor

Free (Synagogue €0.30)

Whitewashed maze of narrow lanes with flower-draped balconies. Medieval Synagogue (one of only three surviving in Spain) and Casa de Sefarad museum (€4).

Activity

Semana Santa Processions 📍

★★★★★ UNESCO cultural heritage

Free · Apr 2–5 · Evenings from ~20:00

Ancient brotherhoods (cofradías) carry hand-carved floats through candlelit streets to the Mezquita. Jueves Santo (Apr 2) and Viernes Santo (Apr 3) have the most dramatic nighttime processions. A once-in-a-lifetime atmosphere. Check routes at semana-santa-cordoba.com.

Pregnancy tip: stake out a spot early — floats pass slowly so minimal standing required. Avoid crowds at narrow alleys.

Show

Tablao Flamenco Cardenal 📍

★★★★☆ 4.4 Google · 4.3 Tripadvisor

€20–25 · Calle Torrijos, 10 · Shows at 20:00 & 21:30

Intimate flamenco tablao inside a 16th-century palace just steps from the Mezquita. Live guitar, singing, and dance — a more personal experience than large venues. Seats fill fast during Semana Santa; book ahead at tablaoflamencoandalucia.com.

Good alternative evening if Apr 3–5 evenings are free. Seated throughout — pregnancy-friendly.

★★★★☆ 4.7 Google

Semana Santa — Holy Week in Córdoba (Mar 29 – Apr 5)

What it is: Spain's most important religious festival. Elaborate processions through the streets every evening, with floats (pasos) carrying religious figures carried by costaleros. Córdoba's old town is UNESCO-listed — the setting is extraordinary.

Key dates: Palm Sunday Mar 29 · Holy Thursday Apr 2 (biggest night) · Good Friday Apr 3 · Easter Sunday Apr 5. You arrive Mar 31 right in the middle of it.

Practical impact: Streets in the historic centre close for processions, often 20:00–midnight. Allow extra time to cross the old town. Restaurants are fully booked weeks ahead — book NOW. Parking near the Mezquita is impossible — use the Airbnb as base and walk.

Tips: Check the procession schedule at turismodecordoba.org. Stand near the cathedral for the best views. Arrive 30 min early for a good spot. The atmosphere is completely unique — absolutely worth experiencing.

Weather & Packing (Late March – April)

Córdoba (Mar 31 – Apr 6)

Highs 18–22°C, lows 8–11°C. Warm, sunny days — often feels like summer midday. Cool evenings require a layer. April can bring brief afternoon showers.

Almuñécar — Costa Tropical (Apr 6–12)

Highs 20–23°C, lows 13–15°C. The Costa Tropical microclimate means more sun and less rain than the rest of Andalusia. Sea ~17°C — refreshing.

Alfarnatejo — Mountains (Apr 12–17)

Highs 14–17°C, lows 5–8°C. Mountain air is crisp. Pack a proper jacket for evenings on the terrace. Nights can be cold.

Pregnancy — Weeks 19–23

Nearest hospitals: Córdoba: Hospital Universitario Reina Sofía, Av. Menéndez Pidal (+34 957 01 00 00) — ~10 min from Airbnb. Almuñécar: Hospital de la Axarquía, Vélez-Málaga (+34 951 06 52 00) — 25 min. Alfarnatejo: Same hospital in Vélez-Málaga — 35 min.

EHIC card: Bring it. Covers emergency care in Spain at no cost. Keep it accessible in your bag, not packed in luggage.

Food: Avoid raw seafood (gazpacho and salmorejo are fully safe — both cooked/processed). Skip soft unpasteurized cheeses. Jamón ibérico: technically a grey area; cured for 18+ months so most consider it safe. Well-cooked fish, grilled meats, vegetables — all fine. Spanish tap water is safe to drink.

Mobility: Córdoba's old town has uneven cobblestones — flat shoes essential. Plan short walks (2–3 km max) with cafe stops. On drives, stop every 90 minutes to walk for 5 minutes. Compression socks for the flights. Rest days are already built into the itinerary.

Heat: April can be warm in Córdoba. Carry water at all times. Avoid midday sun (13:00–16:00) — use siesta hours for rest. Shade and fans are common in restaurants.

Money & Payments

Currency & Cards

Euro (€). Visa/Mastercard accepted almost everywhere including small tapas bars. Contactless is standard. Some market stalls and parking meters are cash-only — keep €50–100 in coins/notes.

ATMs

Widely available. Use bank ATMs (CaixaBank, Santander, BBVA) rather than standalone machines to avoid fees. Avoid "dynamic currency conversion" — always pay in euros.

Tipping

Not mandatory in Spain but appreciated. Rounding up the bill or leaving €1–2 at a cafe is normal. At nicer restaurants 5–10% is kind. Tipping in cash is preferred even if you pay by card.

Driving & Parking

Drive on the right. Speed cameras are common on A-roads. Historic centres have restricted traffic zones (ZTL) — check signs. Park in underground garages (€10–15/day) rather than street. In Almuñécar and Alfarnatejo, street parking is much easier.

Wi-Fi

All accommodations have Wi-Fi. Cafes and restaurants usually offer free Wi-Fi. Free public Wi-Fi in Córdoba city centre at main squares. Ask for the password with "¿Tiene wifi? ¿Cuál es la contraseña?"

Spanish Daily Schedule

Meal Times

Breakfast (desayuno): 8:00–10:30
Lunch (comida): 14:00–16:00 (main meal of the day)
Tapas / merienda: 18:00–20:00
Dinner (cena): 21:00–23:00 (kitchens open 20:30 earliest)

Siesta & Shop Hours

Many smaller shops close 14:00–17:00. Supermarkets and tourist shops stay open. Museums often close on Mondays. Use 13:00–16:00 for rest, naps, or indoor activities.

Free Entry Times

Mezquita-Catedral: Mon–Sat 8:30–9:30 (free)
Alcázar gardens: Free mornings (check current times)
Medina Azahara: Free for EU citizens
Most parks: Always free

Easter Week Closures

Good Friday (Apr 3) and Easter Sunday (Apr 5) — many shops, banks, and some museums closed. Supermarkets may have reduced hours. Restaurants are open but fully booked well in advance.
